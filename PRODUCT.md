# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Usable speaks to small and medium organisations, regional public bodies, consultancies, AI-curious knowledge workers, and technical operators who already use AI but need a proper home for shared context. The primary buyer is often a director, manager, or team lead who needs plain words they can repeat internally: the organisation owns its knowledge, and AI tools should work from that knowledge.

Secondary users include developers and AI builders who want an MCP-native memory layer without building RAG infrastructure, and privacy-conscious organisations that need AI productivity without sending sensitive data into uncontrolled systems.

## Product Purpose

Usable is the organisation-owned knowledge base and memory layer for AI tools. It stores fragments, decisions, policies, procedures, and reusable context in workspaces so AI agents can retrieve the right organisational knowledge across sessions, tools, and teammates.

The landing page must make the value clear in seconds: your own knowledge base, your organisation's memory, under your control. Success means visitors understand that Usable is not a wiki, vector database, or static document store. It is governed organisational memory that AI tools can use.

## Brand Personality

Direct, trustworthy, practical, slightly provocative. The voice is plainspoken enough for non-technical leaders, precise enough for technical operators, and confident without sounding like enterprise security theatre.

The brand should feel like a better place for knowledge to live: calm, owned, durable, and ready for real work. It can be playful through the Usable nudibranch mascot and blue/orange identity, but the product promise stays serious.

## Anti-references

Do not lead with MCP, RAG, vector embeddings, compliance frameworks, architecture diagrams, or long feature lists above the fold.

Do not imply Usable thinks, reasons, or decides. Usable is storage, retrieval, structure, governance, and memory. Intelligence belongs to the humans and AI tools using it.

Avoid generic enterprise wiki aesthetics, AI-infrastructure jargon pages, and feature-wrapper messaging. Avoid positioning Usable as a raw vector database, static document repository, personal note app, or single-LLM tool.

## Design Principles

Lead with ownership: the user's knowledge belongs to them.

Make the non-technical explanation repeatable: visitors should be able to explain Usable to a colleague after one pass.

Show credibility through real organisations and practical workflows, not abstract claims.

Keep technical depth available, but keep it below the core value proposition.

Use the brand mascot, sea texture, orange, and blue as memory cues, not as decoration that competes with comprehension.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA or better. Maintain keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic headings, color contrast, reduced-motion support, and mobile-first layouts.

The brand serves non-native English speakers and Nordic/Baltic organisations, so copy should stay concrete, short, and translation-friendly.
